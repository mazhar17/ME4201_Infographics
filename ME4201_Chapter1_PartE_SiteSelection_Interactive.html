Universal vs. Technology-Specific Factors

The framing

Selecting an optimal location is a complex engineering decision requiring systematic evaluation of many interdependent factors — some shared by every plant, and some that vary sharply with the generation technology.

Universal Siting Considerations

🔌

Resource & infrastructure proximity

Access to fuel or water, grid connectivity, and transportation infrastructure.

🌿

Environmental & regulatory compliance

Every plant must meet environmental regulations and waste-management protocols.

🏞️

Land & socio-economic factors

Suitable topography, stable land, and careful management of community impact.

Technology-Specific Locational Imperatives

💧

Hydro — water head & flow rate

Primarily determined by topography providing high potential energy and consistent water flow.

🏭

Thermal — fuel source & waste disposal

Proximity to fuel (e.g. coal mines) and viable ash-disposal areas.

🔥

Gas & combined-cycle — gas supply & ambient temp

Direct access to gas pipelines; efficiency is sensitive to air temperature.

☢️

Nuclear — seismic stability & population

Low seismic activity and a safe distance from dense population centres.

💧 Water Head & Dependable Flow

Siting is dictated by topography that provides high potential energy (head) and consistent flow. Firm power is estimated from the Flow-Duration Curve at 90–95% dependability across 20+ years of records.

The Ten Engineering Criteria — Hydro

Decisive determinant

🏭 Fuel Supply & Waste Disposal

Critical factors are proximity to fuel (e.g. coal mines) and viable ash-disposal areas. The master trade-off: locate at the pit-head (transport electricity) or the load-centre (transport coal). Low-CV / high-ash coal favours the pit-head.

Decisive determinant

🔥 Gas Supply & Ambient Temperature

Gas turbines need direct access to gas pipelines, and their output is rated at ISO conditions (15 °C, 1.013 bar, 60% RH). Output derates with rising temperature and elevation — hot, high sites lose significant MW.

The Engineering Criteria — Gas Turbine

Decisive determinant

♻️ Cooling System & Water Siting

Adding a steam bottoming cycle (via a Heat-Recovery Steam Generator) lifts efficiency to ~55–64%, but the steam cycle needs cooling — so the cooling-system choice drives water siting. Once-through needs a large water body; dry/air cooling cuts water at an efficiency penalty.

Nuclear & the IAEA Dual-Evaluation Principle

IAEA SSG-35 · NS-R-3

Nuclear site selection is uniquely two-directional. Screening criteria rule sites out; suitability criteria then rank the survivors. Every candidate must satisfy both halves of the dual evaluation:

🌍 → 🏭  Effect of the REGION on the plant

External hazards that could cause an accident
  • Seismic & geological hazard — PSHA; capable-fault exclusion.
  • External flooding & tsunami — design-basis flood, surge, upstream dam-break.
  • External human-induced events — aircraft crash, nearby industry & pipelines.
  • Meteorology — extreme weather affecting the exclusion boundary.
⚛️
⇄

🏭 → 🌍  Effect of the PLANT on the region

Radiological impact on people & environment
  • Population & emergency planning — low density; workable evacuation zones.
  • Radiological dispersion — atmospheric & water pathways to the public.
  • Ultimate heat sink & cooling water — reliable decay-heat removal.
  • Environmental & socio-economic — long-term impact & public acceptance.
Screening then suitability: a site must first survive the exclusion criteria (any single fatal hazard rules it out), and only then is ranked on suitability against the surviving candidates. This is why (11) population density and (12) earthquake danger (Nag 2014, p. 12) are non-negotiable for nuclear.

Siting & Sustainable Development

3 pillars → SDGs

Site selection is where systems-level thinking meets design-oriented decision-making. Every choice maps to the three pillars of sustainable development — and to the Sustainable Development Goals.

👥 Social

Community impact, resettlement & livelihoods, public acceptance, worker safety, and safe distance from population centres.

🌱 Environmental

Water quality & availability, emissions & ash/waste, biodiversity & habitat, ecological flow and climate resilience.

💰 Economic

Capital & fuel cost, transmission losses vs transport cost, reliability, life-cycle cost and decommissioning.

Summary of Today's Session

  • ✔Site selection is a systematic engineering decision balancing universal factors (resource proximity, environmental/regulatory compliance, land & socio-economics) with technology-specific imperatives.
  • ✔Hydro is governed by topography — head & dependable flow (P = ρgQHnetη), firm power set by the 90–95% flow-duration point.
  • ✔Thermal turns on fuel supply, cooling water & ash disposal — the pit-head vs load-centre trade-off decided largely by coal quality (Nag 2014, p. 11).
  • ✔Gas & combined-cycle need pipeline gas and are temperature-sensitive; cooling choice drives water siting for CCGT.
  • ✔Nuclear adds population density & earthquake danger (Nag 2014, p. 12) under the IAEA dual-evaluation principle.
  • ✔The same site suits technologies differently — because each weights the siting factors differently (Suitability Lab).
