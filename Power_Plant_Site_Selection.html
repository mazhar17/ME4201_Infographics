Site Selection Criteria for Power Plants

Where a power plant is built decides its cost, safety and performance for the next 40+ years. Explore each technology on the grid bus below, compare them side by side, then test yourself.

General criteria — apply to every plant type
Near the load centerCuts transmission cost and I²R line losses.
Land: cost & bearing capacityCheap, ample land with soil that carries heavy plant.
Transport accessRail / road / port for fuel and heavy equipment.
Water availabilityCooling, steam raising, and general plant use.
Distance from townsSafety, noise and pollution control.
Environmental clearanceRegulatory approval and impact assessment.
Room for expansionFuture units on the same site are far cheaper.
Skilled labourOperators and maintenance staff nearby.
Grid interconnectionPractical tie-in to existing transmission.
